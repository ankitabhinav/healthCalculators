BMI

The Body Mass Index (BMI) provides a simple numeric measure of a person's thickness or thinness, allowing health professionals to discuss weight problems more objectively with their patients. BMI was designed to be used as a simple means of classifying average sedentary (physically inactive) populations, with an average body composition. However, it is only an estimate and doesn’t take into account a person’s age, gender and body composition. For example, a person with exceptional muscle tone and low fat is more likely to have a higher BMI compared to someone with higher fat and lower muscle tone. It’s not an accurate health indicator.

BMR

Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories your organs need to function while you perform no activity whatsoever. You can think of it as the amount of energy you'd burn if you stayed in bed all day. Since your basal metabolic rate is based largely on involuntary functions like breathing and pumping blood, changes in your day-to-day activity don't do much to raise or lower this number. BMR decreases with age and with the loss of lean body mass. Body Fat %

The body fat percentage is a measure of fitness level, since it is the only body measurement which directly calculates a person's relative body composition without regard to height or weight. The body fat percentage is the total mass of fat divided by total body mass; Knowing your body fat percentage can also help you determine if your weight loss goals are realistic. So before you decide that you need to "lose weight", remember to consider that "weight" consists of both lean body mass and body fat. Using this method on a long-term basis (along with photographs of your body) can provide a general picture of how much body fat you have.

Lean Body Mass

Lean Body Mass refers to the mass of the body that is free of adipose tissue. Thus, lean body mass is the sum of the weight of bones, skin, muscles, organs and everything else except fat. Tracking Lean Body Mass helps make sure you are only losing fat and not muscle. Muscle loss is highly undesirable because it decreases your metabolism. LBM is also your set point to determine how much body fat you should lose.

Ideal Weight

The question “How much should I weigh?” is quite a complicated one. All of us have struggled with this query during some point in our lives. The notion of a healthy weight has several underlying factors such as gender, age, bone structure, height, body fat levels, health, muscle mass et al. They all play an important role in determining our ideal weight. This ideal weight calculator is designed for healthy adults and its purpose is to serve as a motivational tool and help you achieve your goals. Knowing your ideal weight is the first step in any weight loss journey and having an objective helps you stay motivated and focused.

VO2 Max

VO2 max (Maximal Oxygen consumption), refers to the maximum amount of oxygen that an individual can utilise during intense exercise. This measurement is generally considered the best indicator of cardiovascular fitness and aerobic endurance. The more oxygen a person can use during high level exercise, the more energy a person can produce. This test is the gold standard for determining cardio-respiratory fitness because the muscles need oxygen for (prolonged aerobic) exercise, and the heart must pump adequate amounts of blood through the circulation to meet the demands of aerobic exercise. Over time, you should see your VO2 max increase as you increase your level of fitness.

Waist To Hip ratio

Waist-to-hip ratio (WHR) is the ratio of your waist circumference to your hip circumference (calculated by dividing the waist circumference by the hip circumference). WHR is a measurement tool that looks at the proportion of fat stored on your waist, and hips and butt. Using a tape measure to check your waist circumference, measure the distance around the smallest area of your waist, usually just above the belly button & to check your hip circumference, measure the distance around the largest area of your hips, usually the widest part of the butt.
